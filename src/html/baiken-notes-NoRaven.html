Stagger Pressure

I should be using a lot more stagger pressure.

What is stagger pressure?
Stagger pressure is basically when you don't force your turn to stop with a special-cancel.
For example, f.S > H-Kabari~H forces my turn to end.
But if I for example, do f.S into f.S I don't end my turn but rather I extend it to "mental-stack" or whatever you want to call it.
Stagger pressure is pretty good for Baiken actually, since she has a lot of Buttons that are safe (not punishable) on block and are either disjointed or have the threat of a mean special-cancel. This makes the enemy scared to press after a stagger, or how I should phrase it, a button that isn't special-canceled.

A way to condition my opponent is to use H-Kabari after a button that I want to stagger with. H-Kabari is like THE tool to make my opponent sit.

Another cool thing I should use more to stagger is 5P > 5P > 6K. Since after 6K the threat of the Tatami frametrap for a full Wallbreak combo is very high.

Neutral (my behated)

While 2H is disjointed, low profiles, has kind of good reach... it is waaay too punishing when I whiff it, it's hella stubby.
Instead I should abuse f.S. It's fast, it's disjointed, and it has basically no recovery and actually pretty good reach.
