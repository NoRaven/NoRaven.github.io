Meaty c.S Same Side

To hit the same side meaty c.S, simply hold 9 (Up-Forward) and immedietly press j.H. If you inputed your j.H early enough you will fall down on sameside, you will have to instantly input c.S for an easy meaty.

Input Notation:

4/6D [9] (Hold Up-Forward) > (whiff) > c.S > Your combo

Combo used here:

CH c.S > 6H > S-Kabari > 5K > c.S > 6H > S-Kabari

Meaty c.S Crossup

To hit the crossup meaty c.S, simply hold 9 (Up-Forward) and delay your j.H. If you inputed your j.H too early you will fall down on sameside and not cross up.
If you delay your j.H enough you will land crossup and have to instantly input c.S for an easy meaty.

Input Notation:

4/6D [9] (Hold Up-Forward) > (delay)(whiff) > c.S > Your combo

Combo used here:

CH c.S > 6H > S-Kabari > 5K > c.S > 6H > S-Kabari

Tatami Sameside Safejump

To make the Tatami Sameside Safejump just hold 8 (Up) while Throwing and immedietly input j.236K once you start Jumping. Since it's a safejump you want to block while falling. This makes it so if your opponent tries to Reversal with a DP or Super you will block their Reversal and they will be hit by the Tatami Mat.

Input Notation:

4/6D [8] (Hold Up) > IAS j.236K > [4] (Hold Back) while Falling

Combo used here:

CH 6H > S-Kabari > (slight delay) c.S > 5[D] > c.S > 6H > 236236S

Tatami Crossup Safejump

To make the Tatami crossup Safejump just hold 9 (Up-Forward) while Throwing and immedietly input j.236K once you start Jumping. Since it's a safejump you want to block while falling. This makes it so if your opponent tries to Reversal with a DP or Super you will block their Reversal and they will be hit by the Tatami Mat.

Input Notation:

4/6D [9] (Hold Up) > IAS j.236K > [6] (Hold Back) while Falling

Combo used here:

CH 6H > S-Kabari > (slight delay) c.S > 5[D] > 5K > 6H > S-Kabari

Youzansen Sameside Overhead Meaty

Normally if you jump and input Youzansen you wouldn't hit your opponent on their wakeup.
That's why you have to Superjump (Quickly press down and then up) to get enough height.
You also have to input two j.Ps while rising to sort of float in the air to stall.
If you then delay your Youzansen Input you can hit your opponent meaty and even be plus on block.

Input Notation:

4/6D 17-j.P > j.P > delay j.236S

Combo used here:

CH Youzansen > f.S > 5H > Tatami --> Route into H-Kabari or S-Kabari

Youzansen Crossup Overhead Meaty

Normally if you jump and input Youzansen you wouldn't hit your opponent on their wakeup.
That's why you have to Superjump (Quickly press down and then up) to get enough height.
You also have to input two j.Ps while rising to sort of float in the air to stall.
If you then delay your Youzansen Input you can hit your opponent meaty and even be plus on block.

There are two Variations for this setup.
Either you superjump with 9 (Up-Forward) to Crossup immedietly and the Youzansen hits normally, like in the sameside clip.
This is the one used in this clip.
Or you can superjump with 8 (Up), which is a bit harder for pad and stick players as you could hit 7 or 9. But it lets you choose which side you want to hit on the fly (making it less reactable).
will be covered later in the advanced section
For beginners, it's advised to first learn the following setup:

Input Notation:

4/6D 19-j.P > j.P > delay j.236S j.236S has to be input from P2 side

Combo used here:

CH Youzansen > f.S > 5H > Tatami --> Route into H-Kabari or S-Kabari

Youzansen Crossup Overhead Meaty

This time from Hold Throw

If you hold Throw (to not tether your opponent) you throw them far away enough to framekill with 5K and IAD Youzansen for an easy crossup meaty overhead that is plus on block.

It's importand to jump with 8 (Up) and not with 9 (Up-Forward).

Input Notation:

4/6[D](Hold Throw) > (whiff) 5K IAD j.236S

S-Kabari Resets

There are multiple ways Baiken can reset, more like drop, her combo to get a cheeky left / right mix when you expect it the least.

One way Baiken can do this is from CH c.S.

Input Notation:

CH c.S > 6H > S-Kabari > 5K jump-cancel j.S > j.H > delay IAD Youzansen

Based on how much you delay your IAD you might hit them same side as you will be too low.
This one would be a so called "Collision Box" Mixup.
