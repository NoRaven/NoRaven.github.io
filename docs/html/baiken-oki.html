Meaty c.S Same Side

To hit the same side meaty c.S, simply hold 9 (Up-Forward) and immedietly press j.H. If you inputed your j.H early enough you will fall down on sameside, you will have to instantly input c.S for an easy meaty.

Input Notation:

4/6D [9] (Hold Up-Forward) > (whiff) > c.S > Your combo

Combo used here:

CH c.S > 6H > S-Kabari > 5K > c.S > 6H > S-Kabari

Meaty c.S Crossup

To hit the crossup meaty c.S, simply hold 9 (Up-Forward) and delay your j.H. If you inputed your j.H too early you will fall down on sameside and not cross up.
If you delay your j.H enough you will land crossup and have to instantly input c.S for an easy meaty.

Input Notation:

4/6D [9] (Hold Up-Forward) > (delay)(whiff) > c.S > Your combo

Combo used here:

CH c.S > 6H > S-Kabari > 5K > c.S > 6H > S-Kabari

Tatami Sameside Safejump

To make the Tatami Sameside Safejump just hold 8 (Up) while Throwing and immedietly input j.236K once you start Jumping. Since it's a safejump you want to block while falling. This makes it so if your opponent tries to Reversal with a DP or Super you will block their Reversal and they will be hit by the Tatami Mat.

Input Notation:

4/6D [8] (Hold Up) > IAS j.236K > [4] (Hold Back) while Falling

Combo used here:

CH 6H > S-Kabari > (slight delay) c.S > 5[D] > c.S > 6H > 236236S

Tatami Crossup Safejump

To make the Tatami crossup Safejump just hold 9 (Up-Forward) while Throwing and immedietly input j.236K once you start Jumping. Since it's a safejump you want to block while falling. This makes it so if your opponent tries to Reversal with a DP or Super you will block their Reversal and they will be hit by the Tatami Mat.

Input Notation:

4/6D [9] (Hold Up) > IAS j.236K > [6] (Hold Back) while Falling

Combo used here:

CH 6H > S-Kabari > (slight delay) c.S > 5[D] > 5K > 6H > S-Kabari

Easy Youzansen Sameside Overhead Meaty

Normally if you jump and input Youzansen you wouldn't hit your opponent on their wakeup.
That's why you have to Superjump (Quickly press down and then up) to get enough height.
You also have to input two j.Ps while rising to sort of float in the air to stall.
If you then delay your Youzansen Input you can hit your opponent meaty and even be plus on block.

Input Notation:

4/6D 17-j.P > j.P > delay j.236S

Combo used here:

CH Youzansen > f.S > 5H > Tatami --> Route into H-Kabari or S-Kabari

Easy Youzansen Crossup Overhead Meaty

Normally if you jump and input Youzansen you wouldn't hit your opponent on their wakeup.
That's why you have to Superjump (Quickly press down and then up) to get enough height.
You also have to input two j.Ps while rising to sort of float in the air to stall.
If you then delay your Youzansen Input you can hit your opponent meaty and even be plus on block.

There are two Variations for this setup.
Either you superjump with 9 (Up-Forward) to Crossup immedietly and the Youzansen hits normally, like in the sameside clip.
This is the one used in this clip.
Or you can superjump with 8 (Up), which is a bit harder for pad and stick players as you could hit 7 or 9. But it lets you choose which side you want to hit on the fly (making it less reactable).
will be covered later in the advanced section
For beginners, it's advised to first learn the following setup:

Input Notation:

4/6D 19-j.P > j.P > delay j.236S j.236S has to be input from P2 side

Combo used here:

CH Youzansen > f.S > 5H > Tatami --> Route into H-Kabari or S-Kabari

Youzansen Crossup / Sameside Overhead Meaty

This time I will cover the more advanded and better setup for both the crossup and sameside Youzansen.

Now instead of superjumping with 7 or 9 you are going to jump with 8 and 8 only. (That's up with no horizontal input)
And you also won't hit j.P twice as this would make it easier to spot the setup for your opponent.

With this method, similar to the "Meaty c.S" setups, your timing on the Youzansen input will declare whether you crossup or hit sameside. What's also pretty cool about this setup is that the Youzansen input will be the same. This means you won't have to change input direction.
Another good thing about this setup is that the crossup Youzansen will be even more plus on block. Because it hits very late into its active frames.

Use this setup once you feel like you, firstly, have the timing down for the Youzansen.
You can also fall back to framekill with double j.P but that will make the setup easier to spot.

Note that after Youzansen is blocked you can sometimes be close enough to be Thrown. And to counterhit the throw you have to press 5P or TK Youzansen.
Sometimes you are plus enough to get a 5K or 2K to counter, but it's advised to just 5P. when you believe they want to throw.

Input Notation:

4/6[D](Hold Throw) > (whiff) 5K IAD j.236S

This time from Hold Throw

It's importand to jump with 8 (Up) and not with 9 (Up-Forward).

It's importand to jump with 8 (Up) and not with 9 (Up-Forward).

S-KABARI MEATYS

As you already know, Baikens tether mechanic is something VERY powerful. Which means we want to basically always apply it (If that suits your playstyle of course...).

It would be pretty cool if we could sort of force our opponent to either block or get hit by S-Kabari on their wakeup, wouldn't it?

Well, it seems like we are lucky!
There are a bunch of setups or rather "routes" to get a knockdown which gives us a S-Kabari meaty.

2H OTG -> S-Kabari Meaty

One such knockdown route would be anything into an H-Kabari~H knockdown near the corner.
After you knocked them down with you H-Kabari followup near the corner, you have to OTG them with 2H and immedietly special-cancel into S-Kabari.

If you don't get a knockdown near the corner, the 2H OTG will either not hit or tumble them too far away for your S-Kabari

Input Notation:

(Anything) > H-Kabari~H > OTG 2H > S-Kabari

Parry Into Fake S-Kabari Meaty

After a succsessfull Parry you can input S-Kabari.

Note that this "setup" is actually fake. Like the infamous 5K > 6P > Sepultura into en.TravÃ£o Fake Meaty Setup from Giovanna, this setup can be simply 6Ped on wakeup.
What's better about this one than the Giovanna setup is that 6P and S-Kabari will simply clash.

Input Notation:

Parry > S-Kabari

No Followup H-Kabari Fake S-Kabari Meaty

From your normal BnB or other Tatami juggles you can route into 5K Dash Cancel Kara H-Kabari and not do the followup.
This will pull your opponent close enough for you to connect S-Kabari after they tumble away from you.

Note that this is also a fake meaty setup and can be 6Ped. The setup works perfectly against DP mashers or people who like to YRC as those will guarantee a 6H countehit.

Input Notation:

... > H-Kabari > S-Kabari

Route used here:

Crossup Youzansen > c.S Dash Cancel 2K > Tatami > 5K Dash Cancel Kara H-Kabari > S-Kabari

SOMEONE IS GETTING MIXED!!!!

In this section of Okizeme we will talk about resets to get some cheeky mixups and setups.
br What is a reset?
A reset is performed when you purposefully drop your combo or even your blockstring early to "reset" the situation.

S-Kabari Resets

There are multiple ways Baiken can reset, more like drop, her combo to get a cheeky left / right mix when you expect it the least.

One way Baiken can do this is from CH c.S.

Input Notation:

CH c.S > 6H > S-Kabari > 5K jump-cancel j.S > j.H > delay IAD Youzansen

Based on how much you delay your IAD you might hit them same side as you will be too low.
This one would be a so called "Collision Box" Mixup.
