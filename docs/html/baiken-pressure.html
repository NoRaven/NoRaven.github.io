PRESSURE

Pressure... oh my beloved pressure hue hue hue :3
Baiken's pressure has a lot of depth to it, this mostly comes from her many many mixup and strike/throw options.
There are a lot of layers to her pressure so it's best to figure out your gameplan to get an idea on what she wants to achieve through her pressure.

For beginners, I can mostly say to not worry too much about opening up your opponent as they WILL fuck up and in the meantime you built up so much tension and RISC that they will explode on your next hit.
Make sure to occasionally sneak in a throw or two to make your opponent engage and challange into your pressure.

Some general tips for pressure:
- You always want to mix up your pressure!
- Don't be predictable!
- Be unpredictable!
- Mental-Stack your opponent to mental-retardness!
- Make your opponent wish they never picked up guilt gear!

Stagger Pressure

What is Stagger Pressure?
You know how you can gatling your buttons into eachother?
Yeah, basically you don't want to do that xD
With that I mean you don't want to delay your gatlings... no no no, that is gatling pressure. I want you to just press f.S > f.S or something like c.S > 5P, c.S > 5K, 5P > 5P > 5K > 6K or maybe just your standard c.S run up c.S.

Okay but what does stagger pressure actually do?
Stagger pressure extends your pressure or if you want... blockstring for the duration that your opponent doesn't challange you, since... you know... there are BIG gaps in stagger pressure. Stagger pressure is needed when you see your opponent just holding down back and blocking everything, you want to make them try to take their turn back!

Can't my opponent just mash then?
Yes!
Your opponent theoretically could just mash on all your stagger pressure and if you see your opponent not respecting any of your blockstrings you definitly shouldn't go for stagger pressure as you'll just get counterhit over and over again.
If your opponent is just mindlessly mashing you should go straight back to gatling pressure with your delayed gatlings to frametrap them and catch their mash attempts.

As advice from me I'd tell you to use stagger pressure together with strike/throw and your f.S is in my oppinion your best stagger tool.
Let's quickly go over it...

f.S Stagger and why it's goated üêê

Baiken f.S is in my opinion THE stagger tool for Baiken.
It has:
- Great Gatling options: 5H and 2H
-> to make your opponent respect you after it
- Great Special Options: H-Kabari and S-Kabari
-> S-Kabari especially since people like to block after f.S
- Great Mixup Options: TK Youzansen, 2H and Throw
-> People really really love to hold down back after f.S which enables run-up Throw and a cheeky TK Youzansen

Your main goal with f.S stagger is to either make them block S-Kabari or to hit into your 2H or 5H which then lets you blow them up.
Another good thing about f.S stagger is that it puts a HUUUUUUGE load of mental-stack on your opponent. Because after f.S he has to worry about your gatlings, your special cancels, your mixups and the option you could do the silly thing to just go for yet another f.S üòä

Other Stagger Options that are helpful

As I already said before: Stagger together with tick-throws and general strike/throw is veeeeeeeery good since Baiken's throw game is just... well... pretty good üòà

After c.S microdash and either 5P or 2K to setup a tick-throw which somewhat forces your opponent to show an option which in return lets you counterhit them.
Basically it's RPS

The sacred Plus-Tatami

"A plus Tatami? FYM by that Raven?!?!"

Yes, you read correctly üòå
There are instances where Baiken Tatami isn't actually -3 on block but rather it's plus and still our turn.
Plus on Block Tatami ranges from +1 to +4. You can sorta eyeball it as there is a slight delay to the second hit from Tatami. It's somewhat hard to see but if you get used to it you will be able to tell when a Tatami is plus.
